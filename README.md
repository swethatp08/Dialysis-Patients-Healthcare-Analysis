# Dialysis-Patients-Healthcare-Analysis
This project analyzes dialysis patient and facility data to evaluate healthcare performance, patient distribution, and operational efficiency across dialysis centers. The analysis helps identify high-performing organizations, states with higher patient demand, and financial performance trends within the dialysis healthcare sector.


Dataset:

Dataset Name: Dialysis – I & Dialysis – II
Format: CSV files
Size: Each dataset contains 7,000+ records
Data Type: Healthcare operational and facility-level data related to dialysis treatment services.



Tools & Technologies:

Excel – Data cleaning and exploratory analysis,dashboard creation
SQL – Data querying and aggregation
Power BI – Interactive dashboard creation
Tableau – Data visualization and reporting


Data Cleaning:

Performed multiple data preprocessing steps to ensure data quality:
Removed duplicate and irrelevant records
Handled missing values
Mean imputation for numerical fields
Mode imputation for categorical fields
Fixed formatting inconsistencies
Validated dataset for errors and anomalies
These steps ensured the dataset was accurate, consistent, and analysis-ready.


Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

KPI 1: Number of Patients:

States with the highest number of dialysis patients:
California, Texas, Florida, New York
Chain organizations with higher patient counts also have more dialysis stations, indicating higher treatment capacity.



KPI 2: Profit vs Non-Profit Facilities:

Total Profit Facilities: 6,854
Total Non-Profit Facilities: 869
Top chain organizations: Davita: 2,907 facilities (highest)
Dialysis Clinic Chain Org: 258 (lowest among top chains)
Organizations with higher profits tend to provide higher-quality services and advanced treatments.



KPI 3: Chain Organization Performance Score:

Top-performing organizations based on Total Performance Score:
Fresenius Medical Care : Davita
These organizations show strong performance across: Patient satisfaction, Clinical quality, Financial efficiency
Organizations needing improvement: Atlantis Healthcare Group, Belmont Court Dialysis Centre.



KPI 4: Dialysis Stations Statistics:

Top states with the highest number of dialysis stations: California, Texas, New York, Florida, Georgia
These states have: Higher healthcare infrastructure, Greater dialysis treatment demand
Market leaders: Davita, Fresenius Medical Care
Together they hold ~73% market share in dialysis services.



KPI 5: Category Analysis:

Patient Hospitalization Category shows the highest expected values.
SWR Category shows the lowest expected values.



KPI 6: Average Payment Reduction Rate:

Organizations with no payment reduction, such as Davita and Fresenius Medical Care, tend to have higher performance scores.
States with higher payment reductions include: Texas, California, Florida, Georgia.



Key Insights:

California, Texas, Florida, and New York have the highest patient demand, indicating a need for increased healthcare infrastructure.
Davita and Fresenius Medical Care dominate the dialysis healthcare market with strong operational performance.
Facilities with higher profits tend to deliver better quality healthcare services.
Some organizations, such as Atlantis Healthcare Group and Belmont Court Dialysis Centre, show lower performance metrics and require improvement.
Dialysis station distribution closely follows patient demand across states.




Business Recommendations:

Increase dialysis facilities in high-demand states such as California and Texas.
Improve operational efficiency in underperforming chain organizations.
Monitor payment reduction policies to maintain financial sustainability.
Expand dialysis stations in areas with growing patient demand.
